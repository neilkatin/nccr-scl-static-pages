Here’s what you can expect from the American Red Cross

It is our goal to help you begin your path to recovery and to ensure that you are treated with respect and kindness.

You can expect the following assistance:

  • Red Cross will provide modest financial assistance to use towards food, clothing, temporary shelter and other immediate needs.
  • A caseworker will call you in the next day or two to check in as you begin your recovery.
  • A volunteer member of the recovery team will work with you for the next several weeks to guide you through additional community resources that might be available.
  • Referrals to local agencies will be tailored to your specific needs.
  • Referrals will be made to Red Cross professionals to provide the following services if needed
    • Health services to assist with medical, prescriptions or vision needs
    • Disaster counsel support services in talking through your loss.
    • Support services for families, loved ones and/or pets who are missing, injured, or deceased.

All Red Cross assistance is free and does not require reimbursement. The money you are receiving comes from donations from the American public.

Red Cross is not a government agency and depends on volunteers to provide assistance.

We strive for excellence and hope our support will help you as you begin your recovery from this recent tragedy. Please refer to the information in your recovery packet for information on recovery. It’s our honor to support you.
